STAT 309 - Introduction to Probability and Mathematical Statistics I

Probability and combinatorial methods, discrete and continuous, univariate and multivariate distributions, expected values, moments, normal distribution and derived distributions, estimation.

STAT 309 is one of my favorite courses this semester at UW-Madison. This class introduces key concepts in Probability, focusing on the fundamentals of probability theory and how we could use it to analyze given data.

Main Topics Covered

Probability Theory
Independence and Conditioning
Discrete and Continuous Random Variables
